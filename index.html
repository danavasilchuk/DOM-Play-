About this Script

This webpage showcases a condensed version of the famous dialogue between Romeo and Juliet from the play "Romeo and Juliet" by William Shakespeare.

Each line of dialogue is attributed to the respective character using the data-actor attribute, allowing for dynamic rendering or further manipulation if needed.

To enhance the reading experience, you can interact with the script by clicking on the names of the characters (actors). When you click on an actor's name, the lines spoken by that specific actor will be highlighted. This allows you to focus on the dialogue of individual characters and easily distinguish their lines from the rest of the text.

For example, if you click on Romeo's name, only the lines spoken by Romeo will be highlighted. Similarly, if you click on Juliet's name, only the lines spoken by Juliet will be highlighted. Clicking on other actors' names will have the same effect, highlighting only their respective lines.

This highlighting feature helps you to navigate the dialogue and better understand the conversation between Romeo and Juliet, emphasizing the distinction between their spoken lines and enhancing readability.

Romeo and Juliet Script

Romeo: But soft! What light through yonder window breaks?

Juliet: O Romeo, Romeo! Wherefore art thou Romeo?

Romeo: It is the East, and Juliet is the sun.

Juliet: Deny thy father and refuse thy name.

Romeo: Henceforth, call me Love.

Juliet: 'Tis but thy name that is my enemy.

Romeo: Shall I hear more, or shall I speak at this?

Juliet: What's in a name? That which we call a rose by any other word would smell as sweet.
